A Multi-Sensor Interface to Improve the Teaching and Learning Experience in Arc Welding Training Tasks

Traditional methods to acquire hand-eye welding coordination skills are typically conducted through one-to-one instruction where trainees/trainers must wear protective helmets and conduct several hands-on tests with metal workpieces. This approach is inefficient as the harmful light emitted from the electric arc impedes the close monitoring of the welding process.
We present a new automated multi-sensor extended reality platform for arc welding training. Our innovative platform provides trainees with a “bot trainer”, virtual cues of the seam geometry, automatic spot tracking, and a performance score.
Publication:
H.-Y. Lee, P. Zhou, Jiangliu Wang, V. Wu, and D. Navarro-Alarcon. A Multi-Sensor Interface to Improve the Teaching and Learning Experience in Arc Welding Training Tasks. (preprint), 2021 Link
